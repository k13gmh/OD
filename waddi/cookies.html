Cookie and Visitor Counter Policy

Last updated: 30 May 2025

This policy explains how our GitHub Pages site, **Driving Instructor Search** (https://k13gmh.github.io/OD/waddi/index.html), uses certain technologies, specifically a visitor counter, to understand how our content is accessed and to enhance your experience. We are committed to protecting your privacy and complying with relevant data protection laws, including the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the ePrivacy Directive (also known as the "Cookie Law").

1. What are Cookies and Similar Technologies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They're used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to site owners.

Similar technologies include "local storage" or "session storage" in your browser. These work like cookies by letting websites store or access information directly in your browser. Even if not strictly "cookies," they fall under the same legal requirements for informing users and getting consent in many places, including the UK and EU.

2. Technologies We Use on This Page

On this `index.html` page, which helps you search for instructors, we use two categories of technologies:

a. Strictly Necessary Technologies (No Consent Required)

These are essential for the core functionality of our page. They enable you to search for instructors and display results. Without them, the page simply wouldn't work as intended. We don't need your consent for these, but we want you to be aware of them. These typically include:

  • Technologies that remember your search queries during a session.
  • Technologies that help the search interface function smoothly.

These do not collect any personal data and are deleted when you close your browser session.

b. Non-Essential Technologies (Consent Required: The `hits.sh` Visitor Counter)

We also use a simple, free visitor counter provided by **`hits.sh`**. This tool helps us understand how many people visit this specific page and estimate how many unique individuals have used it.

Purpose of the Counter:

  • To track the total number of times our instructor search page has been viewed.
  • To estimate the number of unique individuals who have visited this specific page.
  • To provide us with basic, anonymized statistics to understand the general popularity and reach of our content.
  • The counter has been configured to **start counting from 700** for display purposes.

How `hits.sh` Works (and its use of technologies):

When you visit our page, a request is made to the `hits.sh` service to update the counter. According to their documentation, this service:

  • Counts every page hit.
  • **Does not store sensitive personal information** such as your full IP address, specific browser headers, or any other data that could directly identify you.
  • To help distinguish between repeat visits from the same user and new unique visitors, the service may process parts of request information into hashed (anonymized) data. This hashed data might be stored in your browser's local cache or local storage. This allows the counter to increment the "unique visitor" count without creating a personal profile of you.

Because this process involves storing or accessing information (even hashed data for uniqueness) in your browser's local storage, it falls under the scope of the ePrivacy Directive. Therefore, we ask for your explicit consent to use this technology.

We do not use this visitor counter for targeted advertising, behavioral profiling, or share its data with third parties for their marketing purposes. The information is used solely for our internal understanding of website engagement.

3. Your Choices and Consent

Your privacy matters to us. When you first visit our site, a cookie consent banner will appear. This banner lets you make informed choices about our use of the visitor counter.

  • Accepting the counter: By clicking "Accept" on the banner, you explicitly consent to the use of the `hits.sh` visitor counter as described in this policy.
  • Declining the counter: By clicking "Decline" or equivalent on the banner, we will try our best not to load the `hits.sh` counter on your device. Please note that for static GitHub Pages sites, fully preventing all initial loading might require client-side scripting and may not be immediate, but we will respect your choice as effectively as possible. Declining won't affect the core functionality (like searching for instructors) or content of this page.

Managing Browser Settings:

You can also manage or delete information stored in your browser's local storage or cache, and control cookie settings, directly through your web browser's preferences. Check your browser's help documentation for instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Apple Safari
  • (And for other browsers, check their support pages)
Be aware that disabling certain types of storage technologies might impact the functionality of some websites.
